Wedding Catering

Menu Selection:
  • Collaborate with the catering company to create a diverse menu.
  • Include appetizers, main courses, sides, desserts, and beverages.
Tasting Session:
  • Schedule a tasting session with the caterer to finalize menu choices.
  • Discuss adjustments to flavors and presentation during the tasting.
Service Style:
  • Choose a service style: plated sit-down, buffet, family-style, or stations.
  • The service style can enhance the overall atmosphere of the wedding.
Budget Consideration:
  • Determine a budget and communicate it clearly to the caterer.
  • Inquire about pricing structures, including per-person costs and service charges.
Coordination:
  • Coordinate with the caterer and venue for setup, service, and cleanup.
  • Discuss details like table arrangements, tableware, and decorations.
Alcohol and Beverages:
  • Decide on an open bar, limited bar, or signature cocktails.
  • Discuss beverage packages, including alcoholic and non-alcoholic options.
Staffing:
  • Inquire about the number of staff the caterer will provide.
  • Ensure staff are experienced in catering weddings and serving guests.

Corporate Catering

Menu Selection:
  • Collaborate with the catering company to design a menu suitable for corporate events.
  • Include options for breakfast, lunch, snacks, and beverages.
Tasting Session:
  • Schedule a tasting session with the caterer to finalize menu choices.
  • Discuss customization of dishes to match the event's theme or preferences.
Service Style:
  • Select a service style: buffet, plated meals, boxed lunches, or interactive food stations.
  • Consider the style that suits the event's atmosphere and goals.
Budget Consideration:
  • Establish a budget for the corporate catering and communicate it clearly.
  • Inquire about pricing options, additional charges, and payment terms.
Coordination:
  • Coordinate with the caterer to ensure smooth setup, service, and cleanup.
  • Discuss event schedule, setup arrangements, and required amenities.
Special Requirements:
  • Plan for dietary restrictions and allergies of attendees.
  • Communicate any specific dietary needs to the caterer in advance.
Audiovisual Setup:
  • Discuss audiovisual needs like projectors, screens, and microphones if applicable.
  • Ensure the catering setup accommodates AV requirements.

Event Catering

Menu Selection:
  • Collaborate with the catering company to design a menu tailored for the event.
  • Include options for appetizers, main courses, sides, desserts, and drinks.
Tasting Session:
  • Schedule a tasting session to finalize menu choices and discuss any adjustments.
  • Ensure that the chosen dishes align with the event's theme and audience.
Service Style:
  • Select a service style: buffet, family-style, food stations, or plated meals.
  • Choose a style that complements the event's vibe and schedule.
Budget Consideration:
  • Set a budget for catering and communicate it clearly to the caterer.
  • Inquire about pricing, additional fees, and payment terms.
Coordination:
  • Coordinate with the caterer to ensure smooth setup, service, and cleanup.
  • Discuss logistics, event timeline, and any specific setup requirements.
Theme and Decor:
  • Discuss how the catering setup can align with the event's theme and decor.
  • Consider options for table settings, linens, and presentation.
Special Requirements:
  • Plan for dietary preferences, allergies, and cultural considerations of attendees.
  • Ensure the caterer can accommodate specific dietary needs.

Buffet Catering

Menu Selection:
  • Collaborate with the catering company to design a diverse buffet menu.
  • Include a variety of appetizers, main courses, sides, desserts, and beverages.
Tasting Session:
  • Schedule a tasting session to finalize buffet menu items and flavors.
  • Discuss presentation and arrangement of dishes on the buffet.
Buffet Setup:
  • Plan the layout of the buffet tables, including placement and flow.
  • Coordinate with the caterer to ensure an organized and visually appealing setup.
Service and Display:
  • Discuss how the dishes will be replenished and kept fresh during the event.
  • Consider adding decorative elements to enhance the buffet's visual appeal.
Utensils and Serving:
  • Provide appropriate utensils, plates, and napkins for self-service.
  • Discuss options for attendants to assist guests, if desired.
Theme and Decor:
  • Incorporate the buffet setup into the overall event theme and decor.
  • Coordinate table linens, decorations, and signage to match the buffet's style.
Special Requirements:
  • Plan for dietary restrictions and preferences among your guests.
  • Ensure that the buffet offers a variety of options to accommodate different needs.

Boxed Lunch Catering

Menu Selection:
  • Collaborate with the catering company to curate a diverse boxed lunch menu.
  • Include options for sandwiches, salads, sides, desserts, and beverages.
Customization:
  • Discuss customization options to accommodate dietary restrictions and preferences.
  • Consider offering vegetarian, vegan, or gluten-free choices.
Packaging and Presentation:
  • Decide on the type of packaging for the boxed lunches (e.g., eco-friendly, branded).
  • Discuss how the boxed lunches will be presented and arranged for easy distribution.
Delivery and Timing:
  • Coordinate delivery logistics and timing to ensure fresh lunches for your event.
  • Discuss any specific instructions for delivery and setup.
Inclusions:
  • Specify what each boxed lunch will include (e.g., main course, sides, dessert).
  • Ensure that each lunch is well-balanced and satisfying.
Labeling and Special Requests:
  • Discuss how dietary information will be displayed on the boxed lunches.
  • Plan for accommodating any special requests from attendees.
Distribution:
  • Coordinate the distribution process and ensure efficient and orderly handout.
  • Provide clear instructions for attendees on how and where to collect their lunches.

Fine Dining Catering

Menu Selection:
  • Collaborate with the catering company to design an exquisite fine dining menu.
  • Include gourmet dishes with refined flavors, presentation, and quality ingredients.
Customization:
  • Discuss customization options to align the menu with the event's theme or preferences.
  • Consider accommodating dietary needs and preferences while maintaining the gourmet experience.
Service Style:
  • Select a service style that complements the fine dining experience, such as plated meals.
  • Ensure attention to detail in terms of presentation and service etiquette.
Table Setup:
  • Coordinate with the caterer to plan elegant table settings and decor.
  • Consider elements like table linens, centerpieces, and fine tableware.
Wine Pairing:
  • Discuss wine pairing options to enhance the culinary experience.
  • Suggest wine choices that complement the menu's flavors and elevate the dining experience.
Service Staff:
  • Ensure the catering team includes experienced staff who understand fine dining service.
  • Discuss staffing requirements for seamless service and guest interaction.
Event Flow:
  • Plan the flow of the event, including timing of courses, speeches, and entertainment.
  • Coordinate with the caterer to maintain a smooth and leisurely dining experience.
